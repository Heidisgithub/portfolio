Courtyard Hansaplatz

Hamburg/Germany

The concept evolves from the existing zones and includes playable and usable areas for kids and adults. A sitting area and movable furniture invites people to the courtyard. Flower beds are integrated into the playable areas and are supplemented in a “garden” zone. Here, people have the opportunity to create a garden themselves. Access routes provide direct access to the courtyard and provide a smooth transition between the various play areas. Disabled access through user-friendly surface, such as concrete paving stone, asphalt, etc. opens the yard for inspection and for playing. Wall elements serve to dissipate the surface water. The elements are covert with wood for seating.


Landscape Architects: Hunck + Lorenz Freiraumplanung
Client: Building cooperative (BGFG)
Size: 2843m²
Year: 2016






Housing Complex Oxpark

Hamburg/Germany

The housing complex Oxpark in Langenhorn is a special housing program to promote common living for people with physical, mental or psychological limitations, which is promoted by the responsible authority. Basically, its distinguished between shared apartments and home community. The outdoor facilities of the Oxpark residential complex are produced in close accordance with the design of the houses and as close to nature as possible. The house-related usable outdoor areas are designed as wooden terraces. Gardens, meadows and fields are available for all residents. The entrance area of the complex is designed by natural stones in different colors. The access ways to the houses are slightly elevated concrete paths like footbridges which are also designed for disabled access. These paths are designed with pergolas with integrated lighting.


Landscape Architects: Hunck + Lorenz Freiraumplanung
Architects: DFZ Architects
Client: Foundation Das Rauhe Haus
Size: 4340m²
Year: 2017
